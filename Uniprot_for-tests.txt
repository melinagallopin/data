CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as component of the RNA polymerase II elongator
CC       complex, which is a major histone acetyltransferase component of
CC       the RNA polymerase II (RNAPII) holoenzyme and is involved in
CC       transcriptional elongation. Association with elongating RNAPII
CC       requires a hyperphosphorylated state of the RNAPII C-terminal
CC       domain (CTD). Elongator binds to both naked and nucleosomal DNA,
CC       can acetylate both core and nucleosomal histones, and is involved
CC       in chromatin remodeling. It acetylates histones H3, preferentially
CC       at 'Lys-14', and H4, preferentially at 'Lys-8'. It functions as a
CC       gamma-toxin target (TOT); disruption of the complex confers
CC       resistance to Kluyveromyces lactis toxin zymocin (pGKL1 killer
CC       toxin). May also be involved in sensitiviy to Pichia inositovora
CC       toxin. May be involved in tRNA modification. ELP2 is dispensable
CC       for the complex integrity and, in vitro, is not required for
CC       complex HAT activity. It is not required for the association of
CC       the complex with nascent RNA transcript. Independently, ELP2 may
CC       be involved in polarized exocytosis. Is required for an early step
CC       in synthesis of 5-methoxycarbonylmethyl (mcm5) and 5-
CC       carbamoylmethyl (ncm5) groups present on uridines at the wobble
CC       position in tRNA. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10024884,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11296232, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11689709,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11904415, ECO:0000269|PubMed:13680368,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15138274, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15769872,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15780940}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the RNA polymerase II elongator complex,
CC       which consists of IKI3, ELP2, ELP3, ELP4, IKI1 and ELP6. IKI3,
CC       ELP2, and ELP3 form the elongator core complex. In the complex,
CC       ELP2 interacts with IKI3. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11435442,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11689709, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12139626}.

CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as catalytic subunit of the RNA polymerase II
CC       elongator complex, which is a major histone acetyltransferase
CC       component of the RNA polymerase II (Pol II) holoenzyme and is
CC       involved in transcriptional elongation. Association with
CC       elongating RNAPII requires a hyperphosphorylated state of the
CC       RNAPII C-terminal domain (CTD). Elongator binds to both naked and
CC       nucleosomal DNA, can acetylate both core and nucleosomal histones,
CC       and is involved in chromatin remodeling. It acetylates histones
CC       H3, preferentially at 'Lys-14', and H4, preferentially at 'Lys-8'.
CC       It functions as a gamma-toxin target (TOT); disruption of the
CC       complex confers resistance to Kluyveromyces lactis toxin zymocin
CC       (pGKL1 killer toxin). May also be involved in sensitiviy to Pichia
CC       inositovora toxin. May be involved in tRNA modification. ELP3 is
CC       required for the complex integrity and for the association of the
CC       complex with nascent RNA transcript. Independently, ELP3 may be
CC       involved in polarized exocytosis. Is required for an early step in
CC       synthesis of 5-methoxycarbonylmethyl (mcm5) and 5-carbamoylmethyl
CC       (ncm5) groups present on uridines at the wobble position in tRNA.

CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as component of the RNA polymerase II elongator
CC       complex, which is a major histone acetyltransferase component of
CC       the RNA polymerase II (RNAPII) holoenzyme and is involved in
CC       transcriptional elongation. Association with elongating RNAPII
CC       requires a hyperphosphorylated state of the RNAPII C-terminal
CC       domain (CTD). Elongator binds to both naked and nucleosomal DNA,
CC       can acetylate both core and nucleosomal histones, and is involved
CC       in chromatin remodeling. It acetylates histones H3, preferentially
CC       at 'Lys-14', and H4, preferentially at 'Lys-8'. It functions as a
CC       gamma-toxin target (TOT); disruption of the complex confers
CC       resistance to Kluyveromyces lactis toxin zymocin (pGKL1 killer
CC       toxin). May also be involved in sensitiviy to Pichia inositovora
CC       toxin. May be involved in tRNA modification. ELP6 is required for
CC       an early step in synthesis of 5-methoxycarbonylmethyl (mcm5) and
CC       5-carbamoylmethyl (ncm5) groups present on uridines at the wobble
CC       position in tRNA. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10024884,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11296232, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11689709,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11904415, ECO:0000269|PubMed:13680368,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15769872}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the RNA polymerase II elongator complex,
CC       which consists of IKI3, ELP2, ELP3, ELP4, IKI1 and ELP6. ELP4,
CC       IKI1 and ELP6 form a distinct subcomplex (HAP).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11390369, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11435442,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11689709}.
